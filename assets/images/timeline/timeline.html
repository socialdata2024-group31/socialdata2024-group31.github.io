— George Floyd dies in police custody, starting a wave of protests
May 28, 2020
— First Black Lives Matter demonstrations in New York. Initially, protesters marched peacefully to City Hall in Lower Manhattan. Later, it turned violent with more than 40 arrests.[1]
Public Order Offenses: May 20 to July 10 2020
May 29, 2020
— Black Lives Matter protests took place around Barclays Center in Brooklyn with more than 125 arrests for throwing bottles [2], Foley Square in Manhattan and the Adam Clayton Powell Jr. State Office Building in Harlem. The daily protests continued in northern Brooklyn and Lower Manhattan until June 2, 2020.
May 31, 2020
- In the aftermath of the Black Lives Matter demonstrations, a series of looting incidents took place in New York from May 31, 2020, to June 2, 2020, primarily in Manhattan and the Bronx.
Burglaries: May 20 to July 10 2020
June 1, 2020
— Looting incidents peaked. The New York Mayor issued a curfew from 11 p.m. to 5 a.m. Most arrests were of black males.
